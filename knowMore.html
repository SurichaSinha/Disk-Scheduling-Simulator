Disk Scheduling Algorithms

First-Come, First-Serve

(FCFS)

  • The simplest disk scheduling algorithm.
  • Requests are processed in the order they arrive.
  • Pros: Fair and easy to implement.
  • Cons: Can cause high seek times (poor performance).

Shortest Seek Time First

(SSTF)

  • Picks the request closest to the current disk head position.
  • Pros: Reduces seek time compared to FCFS.
  • Cons: May lead to starvation.

Elevator Algorithm

(SCAN)

  • Moves in one direction, serving requests, then reverses direction.
  • Pros: Avoids starvation.
  • Cons: Higher seek time for edge requests.

Circular Scan

(C-SCAN)

  • Moves in one direction, then jumps back to the beginning without serving requests on the return.
  • Pros: Provides uniform wait times.
  • Cons: Slightly more seek time than SCAN.

Elevator LOOK Algorithm

(LOOK)

  • Works like SCAN but stops at the last request instead of going to the disk edge.
  • Pros: Reduces unnecessary movement.
  • Cons: Performance depends on request distribution.

Circular Look

(C-LOOK)

  • Works like C-SCAN but stops at the last request before jumping back.
  • Pros: Less seek time than C-SCAN.
  • Cons: Slightly more complex than LOOK.
